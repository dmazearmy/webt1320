Louisiana State Dog

The Catahoula Leopard Dog is an American dog breed named after Catahoula Parish. It became the state do of Louisiana in 1979. It is recognized by the United Kennel Club under the name Louisiana Catahoula Leopard Dog.

The Catahoula Dog is a mix of serveral breads, including the Spanish Mastiff, the bloodhound, and the American Dingo. They where originally used by the Native Americans and French settlers to hunt wild hogs and cattle.
